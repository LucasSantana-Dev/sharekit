# Personal-portfolio — reference anchors

## Anchors (5)

### 1. Brittany Chiang — https://brittanychiang.com
- **Sub-niche:** Engineer-portfolio, accessibility-first, industry-credible.
- **When to use this anchor:** Personal portfolio where the primary goal is employment/visibility in the engineering market. Strong CTA discipline model. Colophon as a trust signal.
- **Visual signature (1-2 sentences):** Two-column sticky-left rail (identity + nav) keeps name and role constant while content scrolls right. Inter typeface, dark navy background, muted neutrals, single accent color for links.
- **What it does that other AI portfolios DON'T:** Colophon credits tools used (Figma, VS Code, Next.js, Tailwind, Vercel, Inter) with clickable spinning Tardis linking to older portfolio versions. Signals craft pride + personal evolution archived, not a template shipped.

### 2. DHH — https://world.hey.com/dhh
- **Sub-niche:** Executive/founder portfolio, editorial, content-driven.
- **When to use this anchor:** Personal portfolio where the person *is* the output (writer, thinker, leader). No portfolio pieces needed; thought pieces ARE the portfolio. Founder-grade credibility model.
- **Visual signature (1-2 sentences):** Single-column reverse-chronological river of posts, each occupying identical real estate. Serif-forward editorial typeface, near-white background, near-black text. RSS as first-class citizen navigation option.
- **What it does that other AI portfolios DON'T:** Bio paragraph opens with verbs (Made, Created, Wrote, Won at Le Mans), not nouns (Senior Engineer, VP). Tells you exactly who runs the page before reading a single post. Topics swing wide (Linux, AI, racing, parenting); voice is the through-line.

### 3. Frank Chimero — https://frankchimero.com
- **Sub-niche:** Designer-writer, essayist, vulnerable transparency.
- **When to use this anchor:** Designer or writer portfolio where craft and process matter as much as output. Honesty about sabbatical/rest is a trust signal. Archive-with-question-mark signals ongoing practice, not completed portfolio.
- **Visual signature (1-2 sentences):** Vertically stacked single-column document with blurred portrait, restrained identity intro, no oversized banner. Sections flow top-to-bottom as a well-paced essay: identity → activities → featured essays → book → archive → CV.
- **What it does that other AI portfolios DON'T:** Archive heading reads "Entries 2009–?" — that single question mark signals an ongoing, uncommercial practice. Communicates humility: "I don't know when I'll stop making things." Featured essays precede full archive (curated first, then exhaustive).

### 4. Lynn Fisher — https://lynnandtonic.com
- **Sub-niche:** Designer-portfolio, minimal, annually redesigned.
- **When to use this anchor:** Designer portfolio where the site itself is a portfolio piece. Very constrained surface area forces intentionality. Versioning as a transparency signal.
- **Visual signature (1-2 sentences):** Minimal single-screen composition: name as dominant wordmark, compact role tagline, six navigation links as numbered list (not styled buttons). No multi-column grid, no scroll choreography, no imagery competing with text.
- **What it does that other AI portfolios DON'T:** Roman-numeral version stamp ("v. XIX") treats the site itself as a long-running, iteratively redesigned project. "Gifs" sits as a peer-level nav item alongside Work and Archive — mix of craft seriousness and playfulness is the fingerprint.

### 5. Tobias van Schneider — https://vanschneider.com
- **Sub-niche:** Studio/independent designer, case-study-heavy, brand-conscious.
- **When to use this anchor:** Design agency or independent designer portfolio where major case studies are the hero. Artifacts grid as a personality signal. Manifesto-as-hero for strong brand positioning.
- **Visual signature (1-2 sentences):** Long-scroll with alternating rhythm: manifesto + studio statement → heavy case study → visual breather (artifact mosaic, essay trio) → repeat. Off-white background, near-black text, color carried entirely by project imagery.
- **What it does that other AI portfolios DON'T:** "Artifacts" grid between case studies — marble skateboard, hand-made sneakers, beard oil collab, drone shots — reframes the site as cabinet of curiosities rather than capabilities deck. Closing "Thank you for visiting the house" lands because the house metaphor has been furnished the whole way down.

## When to use this register

Personal-portfolio register applies when: the brief calls for a portfolio, résumé site, or "about me" page anchored to an individual (engineer, designer, writer, founder). The goal is credibility, visibility in a job market or thought-leadership space, or trust-building. Register does NOT apply to company/team sites, product marketing pages, or internal dashboards.

## What this register forbids

- No circular-cropped photo with glowing aura + soft-focus + shadow halo (AI-portrait treatment).
- No bright cyan, neon green, or saturated secondary colors on dark background (SaaS landing palette mismatch).
- No "Skills" section with tech-logo pills (React, Node, TypeScript) listed separately and redundantly.
- No "Powered by [service]" or "Made with [tool]" credit in footer as primary attribution.
- No newsletter modal, cookie nag, or exit-intent popup.
- No testimonial carousel, logo wall, or user-count badge.
- No 3+ primary CTAs competing for equal visual weight in hero.
- No buzzword soup ("We craft experiences," "synergy," "passionate about").
- No hero carousel, autoplay video, or infinite scroll.
- No vague abstract nouns instead of specific language.
